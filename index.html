CNI differentiators — what sets it apart from all 19 peer organizations
Only library-IT joint-venture coalition. Co-sponsored by ARL and EDUCAUSE, CNI is the sole org in this set whose founding purpose is to bridge library and IT communities as structural peers.
Cross-sector membership. 200+ members span higher ed, publishing, IT vendors, government agencies, foundations, and museums — the most sectorally diverse coalition here, and the only one where publishers and libraries are co-members.
Catalyst, not implementer. CNI explicitly frames itself as advancing, exploring, and convening — not operating services, publishing journals, or credentialing. Nearly all peers have direct member-service functions; CNI does not.
Semi-annual closed membership meetings. High-trust, project-briefing forums — not public conferences. This model for sharing emerging work-in-progress is rare; only CSG uses something comparable, and at far smaller scale.
Privacy, identity management & cybersecurity footprint. Substantive topic clusters (privacy: 94, identity: 70) that few library-side orgs address. Only EDUCAUSE and Jisc have comparable depth here.
Longest-running library-IT intelligence function. Founded 1990, CNI has the longest unbroken record tracking the networked information–research library intersection. Its 35-year program plan archive is a primary source for the field's intellectual history.
